2. CNNs, RNNs, Transformers & LLMs

July 13–15 — Meng

Overview

This module is a tour of the architectures behind modern AI: convolutional neural networks for images, recurrent neural networks for sequences, and the transformer architecture that underlies today's large language models. Each day pairs a core architectural idea with a hands-on notebook.

Learning Objectives

  • Explain how convolution and pooling layers extract spatial features from images
  • Train a CNN image classifier and visualize learned filters and feature maps
  • Explain how recurrence allows networks to process sequential data
  • Train a simple RNN for a sequence task (text or time series)
  • Explain the self-attention mechanism and why transformers replaced RNNs for many tasks
  • Use a pretrained large language model and practice basic prompt engineering

Day-by-Day Activities

Day 1 (Jul 13) — Convolutional Neural Networks

How do machines "see" images?

  • Convolution and pooling: build intuition with interactive filter demos
  • Train a small CNN image classifier (e.g., CIFAR-10)
  • Visualize learned convolutional filters and intermediate feature maps

Day 2 (Jul 14) — Recurrent Neural Networks

How do machines handle sequences and memory?

  • Step through an RNN cell by hand on a tiny sequence
  • Train a simple RNN/LSTM for character-level text generation or time-series forecasting
  • Compare generated sequences before and after training

Day 3 (Jul 15) — Transformers & an Introduction to LLMs

From attention to chatbots.

  • Visualize self-attention weights on a short sentence
  • Load a small pretrained transformer with Hugging Face and run inference
  • Prompt engineering basics: zero-shot vs. few-shot prompts, and how wording changes outputs
  • Group discussion: what can — and can't — today's LLMs do well?
